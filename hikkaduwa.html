Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery

Introduction

The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is a popular tourist attraction located in the coastal town of Hikkaduwa in Sri Lanka. It is dedicated to the conservation and protection of sea turtles, which are an endangered species. The primary goal of the hatchery is to increase the survival rates of sea turtle eggs and hatchlings by providing a safe environment for their incubation and early growth. Visitors to the hatchery have the opportunity to see the tiny hatchlings up close and learn about the different species of sea turtles found in Sri Lanka. The hatchery also educates visitors about the importance of sea turtle conservation and the threats faced by these magnificent creatures. Visiting the hatchery provides a unique opportunity to observe and support the conservation of sea turtles, which is an educational and rewarding experience.
