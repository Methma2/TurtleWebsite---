Introduction for turtles

The turtle population in Sri Lanka has intriguing nesting habits and is dependent on particular ecosystems to survive. In order to lay their eggs, female turtles automatically return to the beaches where they were born. They carefully carve out deep nests in the sand, place their eggs inside, then carefully cover them before heading back to the water. These nesting locations are essential refuges for the turtle population's survival. For turtles, selecting a nesting location is crucial. Sri Lankan beaches offer the ideal environment for successful hatching, including the right temperature and sand texture. Female turtles can safely lay their eggs in certain coastal locations because to a balance between accessibility and protection. The existence of Sri Lankan birds depends on protecting and sustaining these nesting places.



































Turtle features Physical appearance Where to find them
Leatherback Sea Turtle (Dermochelys coriacea)

The largest sea turtle species, the deepest divers and the true world travelers, leatherbacks shatter every record. West Pacific leatherbacks can make a 7,000 mile migratory journey within 10-12 months.
Green Sea Turtle (Chelonia mydas)

A diet primarily consisting of algae diet makes their internal fat stores green! The Black Sea Turtle (Chelonia mydas agassizii) is a subspecies of green turtle found in the East Pacific.
Flatback Sea Turtle (Natator depressus)

Flatbacks are the only sea turtles not represented in the western hemisphere. They nest on select beaches in northern Australia and forage in the waters on the north, northwest and northeast edges of the Australian continent.
Hawksbill Sea Turtle (Eretmochelys imbricata)


Often spotted near coral reefs, hawksbills are known for their exquisite shell pattern, featuring radiant streaks of orange and red. Targeted to fulfill demand for “tortoiseshell” trinkets, hawkbills are critically endangered worldwide.
Kemp's Ridley Sea Turtle (Lepidochelys kempii)


Conservationists brought Kemp’s Ridleys back from the edge of extinction using head-starting techniques. The smallest of all the sea turtles, Kemp’s Ridleys can experience cold stunning when water temperatures drop rapidly or unexpectedly.
