Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

Introduction

In Kosgoda, Sri Lanka, a conservation initiative and popular tourist destination called the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is dedicated to the preservation and protection of sea turtles, especially critically endangered species like the Green Turtle and Loggerhead Turtle. The hatchery was built to secure eggs, guarantee successful hatching, and release hatchlings back into the ocean in response to dwindling sea turtle populations brought on by human activities like egg poaching and habitat loss. Learn more about the various species of sea turtles, their life cycles, and potential dangers. The hatchery provides a secure habitat for incubation while collecting eggs from vulnerable nesting locations and relocating them to protected regions. Hatchlings are raised in specially constructed tanks after hatching until they are strong enough to be released into the ocean.
